A Year In Review  

I turned the corner into my late 20s last week. While 27 is not a traditional milestone birthday, it was a transformational year in my life, and I wanted to share some highlights of the past year with you all.

A New Life in Tempe  

Although I began my 26th year still living in the city that molded the first quarter-century of my life, my life already felt different. After two bittersweet election years, I had resolved to find a way to impact the world beyond politics. My first step involved finding a new professional role that utilized my strengths and changed lives without being overtly political.

Chief Science Officers  

In February 2017 I started a new role building out business and community partnerships for the Chief Science Officers, an organization with strong leadership, meaningful impact, and potential for international growth. It was a wonderful opportunity, but eventually it was clear that it wasn't the right opportunity for me.

The Joy of Creation  

A few months prior, I made a small web application to keep track of ping pong games for my coworkers at Oscar Health. Even though it was a trivial side project, and I was an inexperienced coder, I felt exhilarated when I my peers started using the app. Even as I moved on to the CSOs, I kept tinkering. By early Summer, I decided to become a developer.

Reinvention  

By this time, Erin had already decided to attend Columbia University for a Masters in Education Policy. New York City has a thriving technology ecosystem and world-class software engineering bootcamps, so I analyzed some alumni surveys, applied to the programs that had the best outcomes, and chose Fullstack Academy. We packed up the dogs and our lives and moved to NYC in late August.
